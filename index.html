📖 Checkers Rules

Objective

Capture all your opponent's pieces, or block them so they have no legal moves.

Setup

Each player starts with 12 pieces on the dark squares of the three rows closest to them. Red moves first.

Movement

  • Regular pieces move diagonally forward one square at a time onto empty dark squares.
  • Kings (crowned when reaching the last row) can move diagonally in any direction.

Capturing

  • Jump over an adjacent opponent piece to capture it, landing on the empty square beyond.
  • Multi-jumps: if another capture is available after landing, you must continue jumping in the same turn.
  • Forced jumps (standard rules): if a jump is available, you must take it. You can toggle this off in settings.

Becoming a King

When a piece reaches the opponent's back row, it becomes a king (marked with ♛). Kings can move and capture in all four diagonal directions.

Winning

You win when your opponent has no pieces left or no legal moves available.

❓ How to Use This Tool

Setting Up the Board

  • Click Reset to start with the standard opening position, or Clear for an empty board.
  • Select a piece type from the Edit Board palette, then click dark squares to place pieces.
  • Drag pieces to move them around the board.
  • Right-click a piece to toggle it between regular and king.

Calculating the Best Move

  • Choose who moves next with To Move (Red or Black).
  • Pick a Search Depth — higher = stronger but slower.
  • Press ⚙ Calculate Best Move to find the AI's recommended move.

Executing Moves

  • After calculating, the best move is highlighted with arrows on the board.
  • Click any Candidate Move to see its preview and highlight.
  • Press ▶ Execute Move to apply the selected move with animation.

Customization

  • Use the 🎨 button to switch between dark, light, or auto (system) mode.
  • Open Customize Board in the panel to change board colors and piece style.
  • Use Board Orientation to flip the view.

Force Jumps

The Force jumps toggle controls whether mandatory jump rules are enforced. In standard checkers, if a jump is available you must take it. Toggle off for casual play.
