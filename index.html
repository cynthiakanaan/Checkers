How to Play Checkers

Goal

Capture all of your opponent's pieces, or leave them with no legal moves.

Setup

Each player places 12 pieces on the dark squares of the three rows closest to their side. Red always moves first.

Moving

  • Regular pieces move one square diagonally forward onto an empty dark square.
  • Kings can move diagonally in any direction — forward or backward.

Capturing

  • Jump diagonally over an opponent's piece to capture it. You land on the empty square just beyond.
  • If another jump is available after landing, you must keep jumping in the same turn.
  • Under standard rules, if any jump exists on your turn, you must take it. (You can turn this off with the "Force jumps" toggle.)

Becoming a King

When your piece reaches the far row, it's crowned a king (shown with ♛). Kings can move and capture in all four diagonal directions.

Winning

The game ends when one player has no pieces left or cannot make a legal move.

Using This Tool

Setting Up

  • Press Reset for the standard starting position, or Clear for a blank board.
  • Pick a piece type from Place Pieces, then click any dark square to put it down.
  • Drag existing pieces to move them. Right-click to promote or demote a king.

Finding the Best Move

  • Select whose turn it is under Whose Turn.
  • Choose an AI search depth — deeper is stronger but takes longer.
  • Tap Calculate Best Move. The recommended move appears on the board with arrows.

Playing a Move

  • After calculating, browse Candidate Moves — click any to preview it. Hover to see a mini board showing the result.
  • Press ▶ Execute Move to play the selected move with animation. The turn switches automatically.

Customizing the Look

  • Click Customize Board & Pieces below the board to change board colours, piece colours, piece style, and page background — including fully custom colour pickers.
  • Use the ☀ button to switch between dark, light, or automatic (matches your device) display.
